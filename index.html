Creating an anomaly heatmap

On the right the averaged temperature per month, calculated from Jan 1951 to Dec 1980, is displayed. Next we take a closer look at the tempeartures (average values per month) during the year 1878. The anomalies on each month are highlighted in red - The anomaly is the difference between the actual temparature and the average temperature. If we smooth the averaged temperature curve down to a straight line, the differences and absolute values of the anomalies become more clearer. Next, we color code the anomaly values relative to the lowest and highest anomaly that ever appeared between 1850 and 2020. As the anomaly value is now coded in color, we remove the y-axis and create rectangles of equal height. Finally, we repeat step 0 to 5 for every year and display them all at once in a heatmap to be able to identify trends or outliers. If you hover over a rectangle, the corresponding year will be shown. When clicking, you zoom into the selected year to have a more detailed looked at the values.
